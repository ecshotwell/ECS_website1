Eric Shotwell, Artist Blacksmith

About the Artist

Hi, I'm Eric Shotwell. I'm a professional artist blacksmith, ceramicist, and stone sculptor based in Central Indiana. Over the years, I have had the wonderful privilege of teaching and mentoring many aspiring artists. I love to help people learn these traditional crafts.

My creative journey started with the heavy, stubborn worlds of steel and stone. I was deeply inspired by the incredible work of old European and American blacksmiths. Over time, I fell in love with ceramics too. Now, I spend much of my time working with rich, dark Tenmoku glazes. I experiment with the wild and unpredictable surfaces from wood-burning kilns. I also tackle the intense physical challenge of spinning large Korean Moon jars on the wheel.

For me, the studio is not just a place to manufacture objects. It is a welcoming space to learn and try new ideas. It is a place to truly connect with people. When I am not covered in soot or clay, I use my writing to share my everyday thoughts. I write about the creative process and life transitions. I also focus on how communities can stay strong together. I love getting outside to explore. It is wonderful to find the quiet, natural treasures hidden right around us in our local landscapes.

Artist Statement

"For me, making art is all about trying new things and seeing what's possible. I work with basic elements—fire, steel, stone, and clay—to turn raw materials into finished pieces you can see and touch."

My three-dimensional work is entirely about texture, grit, and transformation. I really like the contrast in my materials. The permanent, heavy weight of forged iron and solid stone stands right alongside soft clay. This clay can shape or break easily. Bringing those opposites together is my way of showing life. It shows both the tough and the delicate parts of our world.

I also believe that making art is a big part of my responsibility to the community. My work has evolved past just making nice objects to look at. It is a way to speak out against division. It helps bring neighbors closer together. I might be working on a large public sculpture like 'Resistance over Tyranny'. Or, I might be quietly spinning a pottery wheel. Either way, my goal is always the same. I want to start friendly conversations and build stronger bonds between us.

Every piece is made with a simple belief. You only build a strong craft through patience, helping others, and hard work. You build a strong community the exact same way.

Together, these writings capture the personal, compassionate philosophy that drives the physical art and social advocacy featured throughout this portfolio. My contact form is available for you to reach out and discuss any of these ideas or explore potential collaborations.
