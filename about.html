Eric Shotwell, Artist Blacksmith

About the Artist

Hi, I'm Eric Shotwell. I'm a professional artist blacksmith, ceramicist, and stone sculptor based in Central Indiana. Over the years, I have also had the wonderful privilege of teaching and mentoring many aspiring artists who want to learn these traditional crafts.

My creative journey started with the heavy, stubborn worlds of steel and stone. I was deeply inspired by the incredible work of traditional European and American blacksmiths. Over time, I fell in love with ceramics too. Now, I spend much of my time working with rich, dark Tenmoku glazes and experimenting with the wild, unpredictable surfaces from wood-burning kilns. I also enjoy the intense physical challenge of turning large Korean Moon jars on the wheel.

For me, the studio isn't just a place to manufacture objects. It is a welcoming space to learn, try new ideas, and truly connect with people. When I am not covered in smoke or clay, I use my writing to share my everyday thoughts on the creative process, life transitions, and how communities stay strong together. I also love getting outside to explore and find the quiet, natural treasures hidden right around us in our local landscapes.

Artist Statement

"For me, making art is all about trying new things and seeing what's possible. I work with basic elements—fire, steel, stone, and clay—to turn raw materials into finished pieces you can see and touch."

My three-dimensional work is entirely about texture, grit, and transformation. I really like the contrast in my materials: the permanent, heavy weight of forged iron and solid stone standing right alongside soft, fragile clay. Bringing those opposites together is my way of showing both the tough and the delicate parts of life.

I also believe that making art is a big part of my responsibility to the community. My work has evolved past just making nice objects to look at; it is a way to speak out against division and bring neighbors closer together. Whether I am working on a sculpture like 'Resistance over Tyranny' or quietly throwing pottery on the wheel, my goal is always to start friendly conversations and build stronger bonds between us.

Every piece is made with a simple belief: you only build a strong craft—and a strong community—through patience, helping others, and hard work.

Together, these writings capture the personal, compassionate philosophy that drives the physical art and social advocacy featured throughout this portfolio. My contact form is available for you to reach out and discuss any of these ideas or explore potential collaborations.
