About the Artist

Hi, I'm Eric Shotwell. I'm a professional American artist blacksmith, ceramicist, and stone sculptor. As an artist instructor, I have had the privilege of teaching and mentoring many aspiring artists. My creative path started in the heavy, unyielding worlds of steel and stone, deeply inspired by the rich legacy of European and American blacksmiths and stone masons. Over the years, my practice grew to include fine ceramics, from the iron-rich depth of traditional Tenmoku glazes and the wild, unpredictable surfaces of wood-fired kilns, to the intense physical challenge of large Korean Moon jars.

For me, the studio isn't just a place to manufacture objects; it's a living space for exploration, learning, and genuine human connection. Alongside my physical craftsmanship, I use writing to share my feelings about the creative process, exploring themes of life transitions, mentorship, and community resilience. Based in Central Indiana, I spend my time creating, teaching, and exploring natural treasures hidden in our local landscapes.

Artist Statement

"My creative practice is a continuous act of exploration and discovery—a framework for navigating both the raw beauty of the natural world and the complex social structures we live in."

My three-dimensional work is entirely grounded in texture, grit, and transformation. With elemental materials like steel, stone, and clay, I aim to transform raw nature into unique artistic expressions. I've always been drawn to the deep contradictions of my mediums: the permanent, stubborn weight of forged iron and stone standing right alongside the fragile nature of hand-built clay. Bringing these contrasting worlds together lets me build visual metaphors for human vulnerability and inner strength.

As an artist, I believe my creative output is inseparable from civic responsibility. My work has evolved past just making beautiful things; it serves as a conscious protest against social injustices and a tool for bringing people together. Whether I'm materializing ideas of empathy in public bronze sculptures like 'Resistance over Tyranny' or finding the quiet, meditative rhythm of a spinning pottery wheel, my art is meant to start a conversation. Every piece is built with a simple belief: true value—both in a craft and in a community—is only restored through patience, mentorship, and deliberate intent.

Together, these writings capture the personal, compassionate philosophy that drives the physical art and social advocacy featured throughout this portfolio. My contact form is available for you to reach out and discuss any of these ideas or explore potential collaborations.
