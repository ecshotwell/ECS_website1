Eric Shotwell, Artist Blacksmith

Sweat and Fire

When I make three-dimensional art, it’s all about the physical work, the feel of the materials, and watching them change form. I take raw elements from nature—steel, stone, and clay—and use my own hands and hard work to shape them into unique pieces of art.

Steel and Stone

Hard, heavy, and built to last—steel and stone have always been my main materials. I love mixing their different textures to explore ideas about strength, permanence, and natural shapes. Much of my inspiration comes from the traditional work of old European blacksmiths and stone carvers. Lately, I've also been using these materials to tell stories about human resilience and how communities can stay strong together.

Ceramic Work

Along with metal and stone, I also make functional pottery and clay sculptures using traditional methods. For example, I love working with rich, dark Tenmoku glazes, which I balance out with the wild, unpredictable surfaces you only get from a wood-burning kiln. My Moon jars are heavily inspired by classic Korean pottery traditions. They are incredibly challenging to shape on the wheel, but beautiful when they are finished.
