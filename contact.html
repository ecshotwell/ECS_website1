Common Interests

My creative practice is a continuous act of exploration and discovery—a framework for navigating both the raw beauty of the natural world and the complex social structures that define the human experience. By pulling inspiration from natural treasures and local landscapes, my work seeks to ground these large concepts into tangible, physical forms.

What is important to you?

Collecting and commissioning art is a significant investment. Your time and emotional investment are a long-term commitment to your goals and vision. Whether you are choosing a piece for your home or stepping up to the anvil to learn, you are dedication yourself to a deeper appreciation of intentional craftsmanship.

When you invest in art, you are choosing to partner with an artist.

An artist whose work spans both physical mediums and sociatal commentary must demonstrate how the physical process reflects the philosophical mindset. This partnership thrives on shared social responsibility, using the intense energy of the forge and the steady rhythm of the wheel to spark meaningful interpersonal discussions.
