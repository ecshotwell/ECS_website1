Social Advocacy

As an artist, my creative output is inseparable from civic responsibility. My work is evolving to demonstrate a conscious protest against those who promote mistrust and division.

Countering Injustice

Art that points out societal issues can challenge authorities to address them. My bronze casting of "Resistance over Tyranny" is an example of this. A fragile but brave soul can muster the strength to stand against oppression and injustice.

Community Engagement

An artist that produces work that brings people together and fosters community bonds is a powerful force for healing.

Connecting the emotions of Awakening to the Physical Form

"Awakening" ties into my physical sculpture work. The themes of engagement, conversation, and civic awareness relate to the physical execution of sculpture and blacksmithing work in three distinct ways:

Materializing Conscious Protest

In "Awakening," I write about "expressing ideas through conversation, writing, and art" as a response to a changing social climate. On this official portfolio site, this exact sentiment is mirrored in his statement on social advocacy, where he notes that his physical creations are designed to serve as a "conscious protest against those who promote mistrust and division". The raw, intense process of forging metal acts as the physical voice for those unexpressed internal thoughts.

Translating Empathy into Physical Form

I feel that a lack of collective empathy fragments our world. I bridge this concept into physical art by creating pieces—such as his heavy metal structures and hand-built ceramic components—that explore the impact of society on the human condition. The contrasting textures of cold, rigid steel and malleable, fragile clay are used as visual metaphors to represent the duality of human vulnerability and resilience.

Moving from Reflection to Civic Action

The text in "Awakening" describes moving past an internal phase of exploration to become actively "engaged" with the world. My physical sculptures materialize this by moving out of private studio spaces to serve a civic responsibility. My dynamic abstract forms and permanent materials like bronze and iron to build objects meant for public viewing and community conversation, ensuring that the artwork forces the viewer to confront the uncomfortable, real-world questions raised in my writing.
