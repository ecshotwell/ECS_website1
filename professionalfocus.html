Fine Arts & Crafts: Earth Elements and Industrial Forms

Shotwell’s three-dimensional work is deeply grounded in texture, resilience, and transformation. By working with elemental materials, his art bridges the gap between raw nature and human craftsmanship.

Ceramic Traditions

Specializing in functional and sculptural ceramics, his work honors historic, high-intensity firing methods. The use of Tenmoku glazes—celebrated for their deep, dark, iron-rich finishes—is balanced by the unpredictable, organic surfaces of wood-fired kilns. His reliance on earth tones underscores a commitment to a grounded, natural palette.

Steel and Stone

Expanding beyond clay, Shotwell synthesizes the structural rigidity of steel with the ancient, enduring presence of stone. This juxtaposition explores themes of permanence, strength, and the delicate balance between the industrial and the organic.
