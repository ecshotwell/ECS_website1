Eric Shotwell, Artist Blacksmith

Social Advocacy

I believe that making art carries a meaningful responsibility to the community. My current work focuses on countering the elements that divide us and cause people to mistrust one another.

Countering Injustice

Art serves as a powerful medium to challenge unfair structures and speak up for what is right. My bronze sculpture, 'Resistance over Tyranny,' embodies this approach, illustrating how a single, fragile soul can find the inner courage to stand tall against oppression.

Community Engagement

When an artist creates work that brings people together, it helps heal old division and builds strong, lasting bonds between neighbors.

Personal Perspective

I maintain a personal journal and blog over at ericshotwell.wordpress.com. While this primary website showcases my completed portfolio, the blog serves as a space where I process my everyday thoughts and philosophies before ever working at the forge or wheel.

Several specific stories on the blog tie directly into my perspective on human connection and community resilience:

  • "October Parting": A personal reflection on navigating grief, losing loved ones, and the universal experience of mourning.
  • "Awakening": A key turning point marking my decision to become more active in the world, using writing and art to start meaningful conversations.
  • "Magical Hands": A retrospective on the life lessons, kindness, and skills passed down to me by an older mentor, highlighting the value of human relationships.
  • "Doors": A thoughtful piece that uses everyday architectural thresholds to explore aging, staying mindful, and moving through life transitions.

Bringing My Words to Life in My Sculptures

My blog posts are more than just text on a screen; they completely shape my physical process in the studio. The concepts of advocacy and connection detailed in "Awakening" translate directly into my blacksmithing and ceramics in three distinct ways:

1. Making a Statement

In "Awakening," I wrote about the importance of sharing clear ideas through creative work when social dynamics grow complicated. In the studio, I translate this by creating art that deliberately stands against division. The intense, demanding labor of forging hot metal becomes a physical expression of those inner values.

2. Putting Feelings into Shapes

When people lose empathy for one another, local communities begin to fracture. I attempt to bridge that gap by designing art that mirrors human vulnerability. By pairing heavy, unyielding steel structures with delicate, hand-shaped clay, I demonstrate how people can be remarkably resilient yet deeply fragile at the same time.

3. Stepping Out into the Neighborhood

"Awakening" focuses on moving past quiet reflection and engaging directly with the world. My sculptures achieve this by moving out of a private workshop and into shared public spaces. By utilizing durable materials like bronze and iron to build large scales, I create artwork that invites viewers to pause, look, and discuss the civic questions raised in my writing.

Together, these writings capture the personal philosophy that drives the physical art and social advocacy featured throughout this portfolio. My contact form is available for you to reach out and discuss any of these ideas or explore potential collaborations.
