Social Advocacy

As an artist, my creative output is inseparable from civic responsibility. My work is evolving to demonstrate a conscious protest against those who promote mistrust and division.

Countering Injustice

Art that points out societal issues can challenge authorities to address them. My bronze casting of "Resistance over Tyranny" is an example of this. A fragile but brave soul can muster the strength to stand against oppression and injustice.

Community Engagement

An artist that produces work that brings people together and fosters community bonds is a powerful force for healing.

Personal Perspective

The blog at ericshotwell.wordpress.com functions as a personal journal of my creative thoughts. It is distinct from my main portfolio site, serving as the conceptual landscape that informs my physical craftsmanship.

Several specific entries tie directly into my themes of human connection, empathy, and artistic reflection:

  • "October Parting": A deeply emotional entry processing personal grief, loss, and the shared human experience of mourning.
  • "Awakening": A turning point highlighting my journey toward becoming more socially engaged, expressing empathy, and using art and writing to foster community conversation.
  • "Magical Hands": A nostalgic reflection honoring the wisdom, mentorship, and creative legacy of an elder, focusing heavily on human relationships.
  • "Doors": An exploration using the imagery of physical doors to reflect on the aging process, mindfulness, and life transitions.

Connecting the Emotions of Awakening to the Physical Form

My written reflections do not exist in a vacuum; they directly shape my physical sculpture work. The themes of engagement, conversation, and civic awareness in "Awakening" manifest in my blacksmithing and ceramics in three distinct ways:

1. Materializing Conscious Protest

In "Awakening," I write about expressing ideas through conversation, writing, and art as a vital response to a changing social climate. On this portfolio site, that sentiment is mirrored in my stance on social advocacy: my physical creations are designed to serve as a conscious protest against those who promote mistrust and division. The raw, intense process of forging metal acts as the physical voice for these internal thoughts.

2. Translating Empathy into Physical Form

A lack of collective empathy fragments our world, and I bridge this conceptual crack by creating pieces that explore the impact of society on the human condition. By combining heavy metal structures with hand-built ceramic components, I use the contrasting textures of cold, rigid steel and malleable, fragile clay as visual metaphors to represent the duality of human vulnerability and resilience.

3. Moving from Reflection to Civic Action

"Awakening" describes moving past an internal phase of exploration to become actively engaged with the world. My physical sculptures materialize this shift by moving out of private studio spaces to fulfill a civic responsibility. Using dynamic abstract forms and permanent materials like bronze and iron, I build objects meant for public viewing and community conversation—ensuring the artwork invites viewers to confront the uncomfortable, real-world questions raised in my writing.

Together, these writings capture the personal philosophy that drives the physical art and social advocacy featured throughout this portfolio. My contact form is available for you to reach out and discuss any of these ideas or explore potential collaborations.
