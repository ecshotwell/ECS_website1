Eric Shotwell, Artist Blacksmith

Social Advocacy

I believe that making art is a big part of my responsibility to the community. Right now, my work is focused on standing up against things that divide us and cause people to mistrust one another.

Countering Injustice

Art can be a powerful way to challenge unfair rules and speak up for what's right. My bronze sculpture, 'Resistance over Tyranny,' is a great example of this. It shows how a single, fragile soul can find the inner courage to stand tall against bullying and oppression.

Community Engagement

When an artist creates work that brings people together, it helps heal old arguments and builds strong, lasting bonds between neighbors.

Personal Perspective

I keep a personal journal and blog over at ericshotwell.wordpress.com. While this main website is where I show off my finished portfolio, the blog is where I sort through my everyday thoughts and ideas before I ever pick up a tool.

Several specific stories on the blog tie directly into how I think about human connections and community:

  • "October Parting": A very personal story where I talk about handling grief, losing loved ones, and how we all share the heavy experience of mourning.
  • "Awakening": A big turning point for me. It marks the moment I decided to become more active in the world around me, using my writing and art to start helpful conversations in the community.
  • "Magical Hands": A look back at the lessons, kindness, and skills passed down to me by an older mentor. It’s all about the value of human relationships.
  • "Doors": A thoughtful piece that uses everyday doors as a way to talk about getting older, staying mindful, and moving through different stages of life.

Bringing My Words to Life in My Sculptures

My blog posts aren't just thoughts on a screen—they completely change how I work in the studio. The ideas about speaking up and connecting with people from my post "Awakening" show up in my physical blacksmithing and ceramics in three simple ways:

1. Making a Statement

In "Awakening," I wrote about how important it is to share ideas through writing and art when the world gets complicated. Out in the studio, I do this by creating art that stands up against division. The loud, heavy work of forging hot metal is my physical way of shouting out those inner beliefs.

2. Putting Feelings into Shapes

When people stop caring about each other, communities start to break down. I try to fix that gap by making art that looks at how everyday life affects us. By mixing heavy steel structures with delicate, hand-shaped clay, I show how humans can be incredibly tough, yet fragile at the same time.

3. Stepping Out into the Neighborhood

"Awakening" is all about moving past quiet thinking and getting involved with the world. My sculptures do exactly that by moving out of my private workshop and into public spaces. By using solid materials like bronze and iron to build large, interesting shapes, I make art that invites everyone to stop, look, and talk about the real-world questions raised in my writing.

Together, these writings capture the personal philosophy that drives the physical art and social advocacy featured throughout this portfolio. My contact form is available for you to reach out and discuss any of these ideas or explore potential collaborations.
