Creativity, Vision & Craftsmanship

Welcome to the studio. My creative practice is a continuous act of exploration and discovery—a framework for navigating both the raw beauty of the natural world and the complex social structures that define the human experience. Working with elemental materials like steel, stone, and clay, I transform raw nature and rigorous human craftsmanship into unique, three-dimensional expressions.

The Mediums: Fire, Earth & Stone

My work thrives on the inherent contradictions of my mediums: the permanent, stubborn weight of forged iron and stone standing right alongside the fragile, malleable nature of hand-built clay. Bringing these contrasting worlds together lets me build visual metaphors for human vulnerability and inner strength.

  • Artistic Blacksmithing: Honoring traditional European forging techniques to transform structural steel into fluid, expressive forms.
  • Fine Ceramics: Tapping into historical, unpredictable processes like iron-rich Tenmoku glazes, wood-fired kilns, and the physical challenge of throwing classic Korean Moon jars.
  • Stone Sculpture: Carver's patience applied to pulling organic rhythms out of raw, unyielding geological foundations.

Art as Social Advocacy

As an artist, I believe my creative output is inseparable from civic responsibility. My work has evolved past just making beautiful things; it serves as a conscious protest against division and a tool for bringing people together. Whether materializing ideas of collective empathy in public bronze sculptures like 'Resistance over Tyranny' or finding the quiet, meditative rhythm of a spinning pottery wheel, my art is an invitation to conversation.

"Every piece is built with a simple belief: true value—both in a craft and in a community—is only restored through patience, mentorship, and deliberate intent."
