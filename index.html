Eric Shotwell, Artist Blacksmith

Welcome to the Studio

For me, making art is all about trying new things and seeing what's possible. I work with basic elements—fire, steel, stone, and clay—to turn raw materials into finished pieces you can see and touch.

I really like the contrast in my work. On one hand, you have heavy, stubborn forged iron and solid stone. On the other hand, you have soft clay that can shape or break easily. Bringing those opposites together is my way of showing both the tough and the delicate parts of life.

The Materials: Fire, Earth & Stone

I use three main materials to bring my ideas to life, balancing old traditions with my own style:

  • Blacksmithing: I use traditional European forging tools and a hot fire to bend and shape heavy steel into smooth, flowing designs.
  • Ceramics: I make pottery by hand and on the wheel. I love using classic methods, like rich Tenmoku glazes and wood-burning kilns, to make unique Korean Moon jars.
  • Stone Carving: This takes a great deal of time and many hours of patience. I work slowly to find the smooth, natural shapes hidden inside hard blocks of stone.

Art and Community

I believe that making art is a great way to help people connect. For me, creating isn't just about making nice objects to look at—it is a way to speak out against division and bring neighbors closer together.

Whether I am working on a large public sculpture like 'Resistance over Tyranny' or quietly spinning a pottery wheel, my goal is always the same: to start friendly conversations and build stronger bonds between us.

"Every piece is made with a simple belief: you only build a strong craft—and a strong community—through patience, helping others, and hard work."

Step Into the Studio

Whether you want to order a custom metal piece for your home, add a new ceramic jar to your collection, or try your hand at the anvil or pottery wheel to learn these traditional crafts yourself, I’d love to collaborate.
